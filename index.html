Project 05
FASTA Parser & MSA Scorer
Bioinformatics pipeline for parsing genetic data and scoring pairwise sequence alignments — enabling phylogenetic analysis of mtDNA and Y-chromosome variation across individuals.
Two-part workflow

Part 1 — FastaParser.py
Reads a cp950-encoded genetic data file and writes two FASTA files — one for mtDNA, one for Y chromosome.

Part 2 — MSA.py
Reads any aligned FASTA file and scores every pairwise alignment using match / gap / transition / transversion weights.

Project 06 · MSc Thesis · Lund University 2025
OriGENE: Cancer Driver Gene Classification
Benchmarking deep CNNs for cancer driver gene prediction — combining H3K4me3 epigenetic signals with mutation-guided attention to classify oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes. Code under prepublication review.
The Problem

Traditional cancer driver gene (CD) models rely solely on DNA mutations. But many CDs are epigenetically dysregulated — silenced or activated through histone modifications — without showing clear mutational patterns. This makes them invisible to mutation-centric classifiers like 20/20+ and DORGE.

Approach

OriGENE is a CNN trained directly on H3K4me3 ChIP-seq enrichment profiles near gene promoters (40,000 bp window, 20 bp bins). An extended version adds a mutation-guided attention branch: 28 mutation features modulate the epigenetic feature map element-wise, letting the model up-weight channels linked to functional mutation cues.

McNemar's Test
OriGENE vs. all traditional models: p < 0.001 (highly significant). OriGENE vs. OriGENE+Mut: p = 0.18 (ns) — epigenetic signal alone captures the core predictive power.
Key finding: OriGENE captured 28–35% of CDs that traditional mutation-based models missed entirely. It does so by reading the epigenetic "bookmark" pattern of H3K4me3 near gene promoters — a signal that reflects gene regulation even when mutation data is sparse or absent.
Cross-Patient AUROC — trained on one patient, tested on another (unseen)
Generalizability: The model trained on Liver_I achieved AUROC 0.849 on Liver_II. Even cross-tissue generalization (e.g. Lung→Liver) held above 0.80, demonstrating that the learned epigenetic signatures of cancer driver genes are robust across patients and tissue types.
Top mutation features — normalized importance across models
What the model learned

All three models ranked nonsilent/silent ratio as the top feature — reflecting how often a gene carries functionally impactful mutations, a strong signal distinguishing cancer drivers from neutral genes.

OriGENE and 20/20+ both ranked silent mutations/kb second. Though silent mutations don't alter protein sequence, a high rate suggests active transcription and replication-related mutational processes — informative for CDs vs. NGs.

DORGE's differing ranking reflects the additive, linear nature of elastic-net regression, which can underweight contextually dependent features. OriGENE's nonlinear attention mechanism better captures interaction effects between mutation and epigenetic signals.
